## Course Principles

This course is guided by the following principles:

### 1. Learning by Doing
We believe that hands-on experience with tools and data is essential to understanding how mapping and geospatial analysis work. The course emphasizes experiential learning through projects, lab work, and collaborative exploration.

### 2. Critical Engagement with Technology
Technology is not neutral. We interrogate how mapping tools, spatial data, and algorithms reflect particular perspectives and power dynamics. We ask: Who creates maps? Whose voices are centered or marginalized? How can maps be used for justice?

### 3. No Prior Technical Experience Required
This course assumes no prior computer or coding skills. We welcome learners at all levels and provide support to build technical competency over the semester. However, a willingness to explore new technologies and troubleshoot problems is essential.

### 4. Inclusivity and Accessibility
We are committed to creating an inclusive learning environment where all students feel welcomed and supported. Please let the instructor know if you need accommodations or have accessibility concerns.

### 5. Open Educational Resources
Course materials are shared as open educational resources when possible. We support open-source tools (like OpenStreetMap and R) and encourage critical engagement with data and information.

### 6. Interdisciplinary Perspectives
Mapping intersects geography, history, literature, art, computer science, and social science. We draw on multiple disciplinary perspectives to understand spatial data and representation.

### 7. Global Perspectives with Local Context
While we examine global mapping practices, we also ground our analysis in the specific urban and regional contexts of Abu Dhabi and the Arabian Gulf. We recognize that different places have different experiences of mapping and spatial technologies.

### 8. Collaborative and Supportive Community
Learning works best when we support each other. We encourage peer collaboration, respectful dialogue, and the sharing of both successes and struggles as we navigate new tools and ideas.

### 9. Ethical Research and Data Use
We commit to ethical practices in data collection, analysis, and representation. This includes respecting privacy, being transparent about limitations, and considering the potential impacts of our work.

### 10. Reflection and Growth
Throughout the semester, we reflect on what we're learning—both about spatial data and about ourselves as learners. We see mistakes and challenges as opportunities for growth rather than failures.

## Diversity and Inclusion Statement

NYUAD welcomes students from around the world and is committed to building a diverse and inclusive community. The success of this community depends on the mutual respect and understanding of all its members.

In this course, we affirm the diversity of our students' backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives. We recognize that students come with different knowledge, skills, and comfort levels with technology, and we work to create an environment where all can thrive.

If you have concerns about inclusion or feel that your perspectives are not being heard, please reach out to the instructor or seek support from NYUAD's Office of Student Advocacy and Wellness.
