## Course Schedule: CDAD-UH 1033EQ (Fall 2022)

**Note:** This schedule is subject to change at the discretion of the instructor.

### Unit 1: Foundations of Maps and Mapping (Weeks 1-3)

**Week 1 (Sep 6-8): Introduction to Course & Mapping Concepts**
- Course overview and expectations
- What is a map? Historical context
- Maps as power: Who maps? What gets mapped?

**Week 2 (Sep 13-15): Digital Mapping Landscape**
- Introduction to OpenStreetMap (OSM)
- GIS basics and spatial data
- Field work and mapping methods

**Week 3 (Sep 20-22): Bias in Mapping**
- Analyzing representation in global data
- Case studies: Missing maps, underrepresented regions
- Introduction to Project 1

---

### Unit 2: Spatial Data Analysis (Weeks 4-7)

**Week 4 (Sep 27-29): OpenStreetMap Deep Dive**
- OSM data structure and quality
- Contributing to OSM
- Analyzing mapping gaps and biases

**Week 5 (Oct 4-6): GIS Tools & ArcGIS Online**
- Introduction to ArcGIS Online
- Creating and analyzing spatial datasets
- Mapping urban spaces

**Week 6 (Oct 11-13): Historical Spatial Data**
- Introduction to Project 2: Zanzibar Gazette
- Historical texts and geospatial data
- Working with R Markdown

**Week 7 (Oct 18-20): Guest Lecture / Lab Work**
- Project 1 & 2 check-ins
- Hands-on lab time
- Discussion of findings

---

### Unit 3: Storytelling with Maps & Citizen Science (Weeks 8-12)

**Week 8 (Oct 25-27): ESRI Storymaps & Web Mapping**
- Creating interactive narratives with maps
- Web-based cartography
- Case studies in spatial storytelling

**Week 9 (Nov 1-3): Citizen Science on Languages in Public Space**
- Project 3 introduction
- Participatory research methods
- Data collection and documentation

**Week 10 (Nov 8-10): Maps for Social Justice**
- Critical cartography and advocacy mapping
- Community-based mapping projects
- Project presentations begin

**Week 11 (Nov 15-17): Project Presentations & Analysis**
- Student presentations of Project 1 & 2
- Peer feedback
- Synthesis and reflection

**Week 12 (Nov 22-24): Thanksgiving Break**
- No classes

---

### Unit 4: Synthesis & Conclusions (Weeks 13-14)

**Week 13 (Nov 29-Dec 1): Final Presentations**
- Project 3 presentations
- Reflections on the semester
- Discussion of futures of mapping

**Week 14 (Dec 6-8): Course Wrap-up**
- Final reflections
- Course evaluation
- Celebration of work

**Week 15 (Dec 13-15): Reading/Exam Week**
- Office hours available
- Individual consultations
