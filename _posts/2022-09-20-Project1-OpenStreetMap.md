## Project 1: Assessing Bias in Global OpenStreetMap Data

**Due:** October 18, 2022

### Overview

In this project, you will investigate how different regions and communities are represented (or underrepresented) in OpenStreetMap data. OpenStreetMap is a crowdsourced mapping platform, and like all data collection efforts, it reflects the biases and gaps of its contributors.

### Key Questions

- Which regions have the most detailed mapping in OSM?
- Which communities or types of places are underrepresented?
- What might account for these differences?
- What are the implications for mapping, spatial analysis, and digital equity?

### What You'll Do

1. **Choose a focus area** — Select a region, neighborhood, or type of place to analyze
2. **Examine the data** — Use OpenStreetMap tools to evaluate the extent and quality of mapping
3. **Analyze patterns** — Identify gaps and biases in the data
4. **Research context** — Investigate why certain areas might be better or less well mapped
5. **Present findings** — Create a visual analysis (map, infographic, or presentation) of your findings

### Tools

- OpenStreetMap website and data exports
- QGIS or ArcGIS Online for analysis
- RStudio for data visualization (optional)

### Deliverables

- Written analysis (4-6 pages)
- One or more maps or visualizations
- Presentation slides (5-10 slides)
- Code or documentation of your analysis methods
